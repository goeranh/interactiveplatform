Revision history

 

HESRi Health Equity Dataset - September 2019 - Version 1.1

  • Added redirect key to Compare Inquities from home page
  • Compare Inequities - Disaggregated Data:
    • Added drop-down menu as category filter (from metadata section - xlsx source file);
    • Added drop-down menu as filter by indicator (from metadata section - xlsx source file) - intervention outside standard HEAT+ naive
    • Conditionally to the filters (e.g. Health indicator), the Select Inequities dimension parameter allows the exclusive selection of the available dimensions for the higher grade indicator
    • Removed filter by WHO Region
    • Modified the order of the selector:
      • Select indicator category
      • Select indicator
      • Select stratifier (previouslt labeled "Select inequity dimension")
      • Select year
  • Compare Inequities - Summary measures:
    • Removed Select setting and Select comparison settings
    • Modified the order of the selectors (see above)
  • In Metadata: added drop-down of selection categories/indicators equal to the selection of the indicator in the compare inequities.

 

HESRi Health Equity Dataset - June 2019 - Version 1.0

  • Adapted from HEAT Plus - Version 2.0
  • Limited release at the High-level Conference on Health Equity, Ljubljana, 11-13 June 2019
  • Loading HESRi Health Equity Dataset
  • Explore and Compare Inequality: x axis focused on the min max range
  • Compare Inequality:
    • Comparisons performed among all countries
    • Two-dimension charts visible according to the section on the left panel
    • Barchart for difference/ratio top-low quantiles with trend symbol
  • Fixed various issues of previous version
  • Creation of an additional section containing metadata about the indicators
  • Hosting on shinyapp.io for 12 months

 

HEAT Plus - December 2018 - Version 2.0

  • Incorporation of interactive visuals (replacing static graphs).
  • Incorporation of a translation feature.
  • Calculation of additional summary measures of inequality for non-ordered dimensions, including
    • Between-group standard deviation (BGSD),
    • Coefficient of variation (COV),
    • Unweighted mean differnce from best performing subgroup (MDBU),
    • Unweighted mean difference from mean (MDMU).
  • Revision of summary measure formulas for
    • Slope index of inequality (SII),
    • Relative index of inequality (RII).
  • Incorporation of additional summary measures of inequality in the Explore Inequality - Disaggregated data (detailed bar graphs) tab, calculated for non-ordered dimensions with more than
    • 30 subgroups:
      • Difference between percentile 80 and percentile 20,
      • Ratio of percentile 80 to percentile 20,
      • Differnce between the mean estimates in quintile 5 and quintile 1,
      • Ratio of the mean estimates in quintile 5 to quintile 1.
    • 60 subgroups:
      • Difference between percentile 90 and percentile 10,
      • Ratio of percentile 90 to percentile 10,
      • Differnce between the mean estimates in decile 10 and decile 1,
      • Ratio of the mean estimates in decile 10 to decile 1.
    • 100 subgroups:
      • Difference between percentile 95 and percentile 5,
      • Ratio of percentile 95 to percentile 5,
      • Differnce between the mean estimates in the top 5% and the bottom 5%,
      • Ratio of the mean estimates in the top 5% to the bottom 5%.
